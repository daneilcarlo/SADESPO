DELICACIES


From Farm to Fork: Experience Freshness and Flavor in Sariaya's Restaurants!



Broas

Ladyfingers are usually a pale cookie with a light and airy texture. They are said to have originated in Italy. It is possible you’re more familiar with them than you might think as they are used as one part of other desserts, one being the extremely popular tiramisu.


Apas

Apas are traditional cookies originating from Quezon in the Philippines. These Southern Tagalog specialties have a base of a typical biscuit, with hints of sweetness and milkiness. They are very thin, oblong, wafer-like, and topped with sugar. The crispy and thin cookies can be bought in most markets in the Philippines.


Pinagong

Pinagong is a traditional bread roll originating from Sariaya (Quezon province) in the Philippines. This dense bread roll is usually made with a combination of flour, salt, yeast, sugar, and milk. Eggs and butter are sometimes also added to the dough mixture.


Pianono

Pianono is a Filipino Swiss Roll, a tender cake roll traditionally filled with margarine and sugar, but can be rolled with your favorite filling! Try this easy pianono recipe with jam, custard, whipped cream, or whatever you fancy! My version is filled with coconut jam.


Tamales

Tamales a delicious, so filling and mouth-watering rice dough delicacy that is sauted in garlic, onion, meat stock, lard and seasonings. It is embellished with shredded meat, hard boiled egg slices and peanuts before being artfully wrapped in pre heated banana leaves and steamed.


Suman

Suman is a traditional Filipino rice cake made from glutinous rice cooked in coconut milk and wrapped in banana leaves. In Sariaya, Quezon, suman is often flavored with pandan leaves, giving it a subtle aroma and green color. It is typically served with sugar or latik (coconut caramel sauce) for dipping.


Sinukmani

Sinukmani is a sticky rice dessert similar to suman but with a richer and creamier texture. It is made from glutinous rice cooked in coconut milk and sweetened with sugar or molasses. In Sariaya, Quezon, sinukmani is often topped with grated coconut or toasted sesame seeds for added flavor and texture.


Sinulbot

Sinulbot is a sweet delicacy from Sariaya, Quezon, made from glutinous rice cooked in coconut milk and sweetened with sugar or molasses. It has a thick and creamy consistency similar to porridge and is often flavored with pandan leaves or langka (jackfruit). Sinulbot is enjoyed as a dessert or snack, especially during special occasions.
